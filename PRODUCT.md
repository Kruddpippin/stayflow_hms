# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Hotel staff (front desk, managers, admins) who manage daily operations — checking guests in/out, assigning rooms, handling billing. Secondary users are guests who book and manage their own stays through a self-service portal. Staff use this on desktop at a front desk or back office; guests use it on any device.

## Product Purpose

StayFlow HMS is a hotel management system that unifies reservations, room management, guest records, and billing into one fast, modern dashboard. It replaces spreadsheets and legacy PMS software. Success looks like: staff complete check-in in under 30 seconds, managers see property status at a glance, guests book without calling the front desk.

## Brand Personality

Professional, clean, trustworthy. The interface should feel like a tool built by people who understand hotels — competent and reliable, never flashy. Quiet confidence over visual noise. Every element earns its place.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS admin templates (the gray-and-blue dashboard that could be anything)
- Overly playful or colorful interfaces (no cartoon icons, rainbow palettes, toy-like energy)
- Heavy enterprise software (no Oracle/SAP density, tiny fonts, or intimidating complexity)
- AI-generated template look (no cream backgrounds, gradient text, or identical card grids)

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity over decoration** — every visual element communicates something. No ornament for its own sake.
2. **Density without overwhelm** — show enough data to act on, but never more than a screen can hold comfortably.
3. **Status at a glance** — color, position, and hierarchy make the state of the property legible in seconds.
4. **Staff-speed interactions** — workflows are fast. Fewer clicks, fewer modals, fewer confirmation steps.
5. **Earned trust** — the UI never looks broken, never shows stale data, never surprises. Reliability is a design feature.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance. 4.5:1 contrast minimum for body text, 3:1 for large text and UI components. Keyboard navigation for all interactive elements. Reduced motion support via `prefers-reduced-motion`. Color is never the sole indicator of status — always paired with text labels or icons.
